## MARCH 2026 PRIMARY SOURCES (New This Rebuild)

### 1. Wix Studio AI Search Lab — Content Types Most Cited by LLMs (March 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.wix.com/studio/ai-search-lab/research/content-types-most-cited-by-llms
- **Published:** March 24, 2026 (via Search Engine Land, March 2026)
- **Methodology:** 75,000 AI answers, 1 million+ citations analyzed across ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity
- **Key findings used:**
  - Listicles lead all citation formats at 21.9% overall
  - Articles: 16.7% of all citations
  - Product pages: 13.7% of all citations
  - Informational queries: Articles 45.5%, Listicles 21.7%
  - Commercial queries: Listicles 40.9%
  - Third-party listicles earn 80.9% of citations in professional services vs. 19.1% self-promotional
  - Perplexity cites discussions and forums at 17%
  - Query intent predicts citation format more than industry or model

### 2. Semrush LinkedIn AI Visibility Study (March 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.semrush.com/blog/linkedin-ai-visibility-study/
- **Published:** March 2026
- **Methodology:** 89,000 LinkedIn URLs cited by AI, from analysis of 325,000 prompts across ChatGPT Search, Google AI Mode, Perplexity (January-February 2026)
- **Key findings used:**
  - LinkedIn is #2 most cited domain, appearing in 11% of AI responses
  - Long-form articles (500-2,000 words) and mid-length posts (50-299 words) have highest citation rates
  - 54-64% of cited posts focus on knowledge sharing or practical advice
  - Reshares rarely referenced by AI
  - Most cited posts: 15-25 reactions (moderate engagement, not viral)
  - 75% of cited authors post 5+ times in 4 weeks
  - Perplexity cites Company Pages most (59%), ChatGPT/Google AI Mode cite individual creators (59%)

## DATA INTEGRITY NOTES

1. The 43.8% ChatGPT listicle citation figure (Ahrefs) and the 21.9% overall listicle figure (Wix Studio) are not contradictory. Ahrefs measured ChatGPT specifically. Wix Studio measured across three platforms. Both are cited correctly.

2. The 45.5% informational citation figure (Wix Studio) refers to articles specifically, not listicles. Both figures are from the same study measuring different query intent categories.

3. The 239% citation lift (Stacker/Scrunch) is a median figure. Individual results vary. The study was conducted by Stacker, which has commercial interest in third-party content distribution. The finding is consistent with Ahrefs' independent brand-mention correlation data.

4. All em-dash occurrences have been removed from all templates. Content uses plain hyphens for compound modifiers and "to" for ranges.
