# Deep-Linking Guide for AI Notices

## Why Deep Linking Matters for AI Citation

Internal linking is not just an SEO signal. It is an AI authority signal. AI systems map topic relationships across your domain. When a news release links to your research page, the release passes citation authority to the research page. When your editorial links to your product page, both pages become more citable for queries about that topic.

Key data: Distributing content to a wide range of publications increases AI citations by up to 325% compared to publishing only on your own site (Stacker, December 2025). Internal deep linking is the owned-media version of that same principle.

Source: Stacker via position.digital, December 2025

---

## Rule 1: Link on First Mention, Not Every Mention

Link the first time you mention a topic in the document. Do not link the same target more than once per document.

Wrong:
"[Private company intelligence](link) platforms have changed how PE firms source deals. Many [private company intelligence](link) tools now use AI..."

Right:
"[Private company intelligence platforms](link) have changed how PE firms source deals. Many tools in this category now use AI..."

---

## Rule 2: Anchor Text Must Describe the Destination

Anchor text is an AI signal about the content at the linked destination. Generic anchor text ("click here," "learn more," "this page") has no signal value.

Anchor text patterns that work:

| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| "click here" | "pre-bid intelligence platform" |
| "read more" | "2026 Dealmaker Intelligence Report" |
| "our product" | "Fieldline subcontractor coverage database" |
| "this page" | "private equity deal sourcing tools" |
| "learn more" | "how private company intelligence works" |

Rule: Anchor text should tell the reader (and AI) exactly what they will find at the destination.

---

## Rule 3: Link to Authority Pages, Not the Homepage

Authority pages are the pages with the most specific, topically relevant content. These are what AI cites.

Authority page types:

- Product pages: `/for-gcs`, `/private-equity`, `/deal-sourcing`
- Research pages: `/research/2026-report`
- Feature pages: `/coverage`, `/data-methodology`
- Comparison pages: `/vs-pitchbook`

Never link to your homepage from within body content. The homepage is not an authority page for any specific topic.

---

## Rule 4: 2-3 Internal Links Per 500 Words

Link density guideline from AI SEO research:

- Under 500 words: 1-2 links
- 500-1000 words: 2-3 links
- 1000-2000 words: 4-6 links

Over-linking (every other sentence) reduces citation probability by fragmenting the reader's path. Under-linking misses authority transfer opportunities.

Source: Stridec AI SEO Internal Linking Models, February 2026

---

## Rule 5: Link to Citation Sources, Not Just Mentions

When you cite a statistic or study, link directly to the source document.

Format that works:
"According to [the 2026 Dealmaker Intelligence Report](link), 67% of PE firms..."

Format that does not work:
"According to a Grata study, 67% of PE firms..."

The linked citation gives AI a verification path. Unlinked citations are less citable because AI cannot confirm the source.

## Format-Specific Deep-Linking Rules

### Editorial/Feature

- Link on first mention of the product/platform name (link to product page, not home)
- Link the first stat mentioned to the source study
- Link one section subhead anchor to a related editorial or research page
- Place links in body text, not in the FAQ section (FAQ answers should be self-contained)

### Listicle

- Link the KEY FACTS section stats to their source pages
- Link the first mention of any competitive or market term to a relevant explainer page
- Do not link inside individual list items (it fragments the item's AI extraction)
- Link the synthesis section to a related editorial or case study

### News/Announcement

- Link the product/feature name in paragraph 1 to the product page
- Link the company name in the boilerplate to the specific product page (not homepage)
- Link the full report or related coverage from the call to action at the end

### Data/Research Release

- Link every stat in KEY FINDINGS to the source study
- Link the "full report available at" line to the research page
- Link the "About [Company]" section company name to a relevant product page

### Expert Q&A

- Link the expert's name in the credential block to their LinkedIn profile
- Link the company name in "About the Expert" to a relevant product page
- Link the first mention of a specific product, platform, or report mentioned in answers

---

## Deep-Linking Checklist

- [ ] First mention of every major topic is linked (product, study, category term)
- [ ] Anchor text describes the destination specifically
- [ ] Links point to authority/product/research pages, not homepage
- [ ] 2-3 internal links per 500 words
- [ ] All stats have a linked citation (not just a text attribution)
- [ ] No duplicate links to the same destination within the document
- [ ] FAQ answers are NOT linked (they must be self-contained for AI extraction)
